# Project Planning

## What are you really trying to do? What are the goals for my project?

I am building **Luma Events Contacts Manager** — a CRM tailored for managing the people in my community and professional network. The core need is to have a clear, centralized understanding of who my participants and users are, so I can engage with them meaningfully and at the right time.

The primary use cases are:

- **Contact intelligence**: Gather and store rich profile data on each person — their background, interests, profession, and role in my ecosystem (e.g., participant, investor, speaker candidate, community member).
- **Event invitations**: Use contact data to identify who would be most relevant or interested in a given event, and invite them in a targeted way. For investors or high-profile contacts, I may also reach out to ask them to speak.
- **Newsletter targeting**: Leverage contact interests and profile data to send more personalized, relevant newsletters — rather than blasting the same content to everyone. The goal is higher engagement through relevance.
- **Research layer**: Be able to conduct or store research on contacts — what they care about, what they've worked on, what topics resonate with them — so outreach and content feel informed and intentional.
- **Data export**: Export contact data in a format that can be uploaded to an external newsletter platform (e.g., Mailchimp, Beehiiv, ConvertKit), enabling a smooth handoff between CRM and distribution tools.
- **Luma metrics integration**: Pull event data from Luma into the CRM — including which events a contact registered for, actually attended, or was invited to. This enriches contact profiles with behavioral engagement data over time.
- **Deduplication**: Since contacts may appear across multiple Luma events, the system should intelligently consolidate duplicate records — merging data from different event imports into a single unified contact profile.
